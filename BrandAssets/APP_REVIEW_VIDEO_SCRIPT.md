# EEAccess — App Review demo video script (~75s)

Purpose: prove to the App Reviewer that the Apple Watch → Tesla key works
(they can't test it without a vehicle), and show the card wallet they *can*
test. Keep it under ~90 seconds. Caption everything — reviewers often watch
muted. Show the build number. Blur your full VIN and plate.

Capture with two devices: one screen-recording the Apple Watch / iPhone, one
filming the car. For the unlock shot, keep the **watch and the car in the same
frame** so cause-and-effect is undeniable.

| # | Time | Shot | On-screen caption | Notes |
|---|------|------|-------------------|-------|
| 0 | 0:00–0:05 | Title card | "EEAccess · v1.4 (build 9) · App Review demo" | Plain text card; add "Apple Watch Tesla key + card wallet". |
| 1 | 0:05–0:16 | iPhone screen recording: open app, scan a QR/barcode to add a gym card, show it, then show it on the Apple Watch | "Add any gym/loyalty card — works on iPhone & Watch, no vehicle needed" | This is the fully testable part. Reassures the reviewer the app works without a car. |
| 2 | 0:16–0:23 | iPhone: tap the Tesla Key icon, type VIN, "Save & sync to watch" | "Enter VIN on iPhone → syncs to the watch" | Blur the VIN. Show the "Synced to your Apple Watch" confirmation. |
| 3 | 0:23–0:45 | In the car. Watch: Tesla Key → Finish Pairing → Start Pairing. Tap the physical Tesla key card on the console. Car touchscreen shows the add-key prompt → tap Confirm. Watch: Verify → "Paired ✓" | "Pair once in your car with your key card" then "Tap Confirm on the car screen" then "Paired ✓" | The core proof. Get the watch, the console card-tap, and the car's confirmation screen all on camera. |
| 4 | 0:45–0:57 | One continuous frame: watch + car. Tap **Unlock** on the watch → the car unlocks (mirrors unfold / lights flash / handles present). Then tap **Lock** → it locks | "Tap Unlock on the watch — the car unlocks" / "Tap Lock — it locks" | Do NOT cut between the tap and the car reacting — single take proves it's real. |
| 5 | 0:57–1:05 | Watch face showing the "Tesla Key" complication → tap it → screen opens → Unlock | "One tap from the watch face" | Shows the complication deep-link. |
| 6 | 1:05–1:15 | Phone visibly left on a table (or in Airplane Mode on screen). Get in, press the brake, car shows ready-to-drive | "No phone. No internet. Get in and drive." | Sells the offline, phone-free claim — the differentiator. |
| 7 | 1:15–1:20 | Closing card | "All on-device · No Tesla account required" + "Not affiliated with Tesla, Inc." | Reinforces privacy + the trademark disclaimer. |

## Optional voiceover (or use as captions)

1. "EEAccess puts your scannable cards — and your Tesla key — on your Apple Watch."
2. "Add a gym or loyalty card by scanning it. It shows up on the watch, no phone needed."
3. "Enter your VIN on the phone; it syncs to the watch."
4. "In the car, you pair once — tap your key card on the console and confirm on the screen."
5. "Now your wrist is the key. Tap to unlock… and lock."
6. "Or tap the complication on your watch face."
7. "No phone, no signal, no account — just get in and drive."

## Production notes
- 1080p, MP4/H.264. Portrait or landscape both fine; portrait reads well for watch shots.
- Burn captions in (don't rely on audio).
- Keep total length under 2 minutes; ~75s is ideal.
- Blur VIN, license plate, and any account details.
- Host the file somewhere stable and put the link in App Store Connect → App
  Review Information → Notes (and/or attach directly), referencing it from the
  review notes in APP_STORE_COPY.md.

## What this proves to the reviewer
- The card wallet (shots 1–2) is real and works with no vehicle.
- The Tesla key (shots 3–6) is a genuine, working feature that simply requires
  the user's own car + key card — which is why it can't be reproduced in
  Apple's test lab.
